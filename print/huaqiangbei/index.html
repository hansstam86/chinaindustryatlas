Huaqiangbei (华强北) is a few square kilometres of Shenzhen where roughly 200,000 merchants sell every chip, cable, sensor and phone part that exists. It is the place hardware is born — where, as the saying goes, you can source a whole prototype in an afternoon. This map renders that density as a thing of beauty: an angled bird's-eye view of the market towers, drawn by hand in the atlas's own cartographic style.

It is the first plate of a printed China Industry Atlas — a National-Geographic-grade book of where the world's things are actually made. Rather than print the whole book on faith, we're issuing it one map at a time. Reserve this one and you fund the next, and your name can sit in the atlas as one of the people who made it exist.
